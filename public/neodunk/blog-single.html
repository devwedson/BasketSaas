Improve Your Balance and Footwork

Good balance and precise footwork are essential components of a successful jump shot. Proper stance and movement allow players to maintain control, generate power, and execute shots accurately even under defensive pressure. Practicing foot positioning, pivoting, jump stops, and quick directional changes helps develop stability and coordination. By incorporating balance and footwork drills into your training, you can improve your overall shooting consistency.

  • Keep feet shoulder-width apart, knees bent, and weight evenly distributed for balance while shooting, pivoting, or changing direction.
  • Pivot on both feet to protect the ball, adjust position, and create open shots. Proper pivoting improves mobility and maintains balance.
  • Stop abruptly and land softly when receiving the ball or near the basket. Controlled stops boost accuracy and prevent injuries.
  • Incorporate side-to-side footwork drills to improve agility. Quick lateral movements maintain balance and create scoring opportunities.
  • Perform footwork drills under defensive pressure or at game speed. Realistic practice improves balance and coordination.
